# Abstract

Precision agriculture demands accurate, organ-level morphological measurements of cotton (*Gossypium hirsutum*) bolls for timely management decisions including plant growth regulator application, harvest aid scheduling, and stress detection. Traditional Structure-from-Motion (SfM) photogrammetry systematically fails on cotton canopies due to the textureless appearance of open bolls, wind-induced temporal inconsistency, and severe leaf occlusion in pre-defoliation imagery. We present a semantic 3D reconstruction pipeline that replaces pixel-level feature matching with dense semantic correspondence fields derived from the DINOv2 vision foundation model, enabling robust multi-view matching on surfaces where classical descriptors (SIFT, SuperPoint) degenerate. Our pipeline integrates SAM 2 for zero-shot boll instance segmentation with temporal tracking, a feature-aligned 3D reconstruction module that enforces semantic consistency in embedding space, and an automated morphological measurement extraction stage. We introduce defoliation as a controlled visibility intervention experiment, demonstrating that post-defoliation imagery yields substantially higher boll retention rates in 3D reconstruction and lower morphological measurement variance compared to pre-defoliation conditions. To close the loop from geometric signals to actionable field decisions, we deploy an edge-native large language model (GLM-4-9B) that synthesizes boll morphology statistics into structured management recommendations. We evaluate on 1,549 DJI UAV images captured across pre- and post-defoliation flights over the same cotton field on a single day, establishing the first benchmark for semantic-correspondence-driven 3D crop phenotyping.
